# Investing in Japan for Foreigners: A Guide to NISA and iDeCo

Living in Japan, you have access to government tax-advantaged investment programs that can make a real difference in your long-term financial planning. **NISA** (少額投資非課税制度) and **iDeCo** (個人型確定拠出年金) are the two flagship schemes — and as long as you're a tax resident (居住者) in Japan, foreign residents are generally eligible to use them. This guide walks you through the key points for opening accounts and things foreigners especially need to watch out for.

---

## 1. Opening a Brokerage Account (the prerequisite)

To participate in NISA or iDeCo, you first need to open an account with a securities company (証券会社). This is the foundation for all investing in Japan.

### Required Documents

- **マイナンバーカード** (My Number card): almost always required for account opening
- **在留カード** (Residence card)
- A **Japanese bank account** to link to your brokerage account

### What to Expect as a Foreign Resident

**① Mail-in applications are usually the better option**

Many brokerages' online identity verification (eKYC) systems don't support 在留カード, which means foreign residents often run into authentication errors. To avoid the back-and-forth, **start with a mail-in application (郵送申込)** and submit your documents in paper form.

**② Your name must match exactly across all documents**

The name on your bank account, 在留カード, and brokerage account must be **exactly the same**. Even minor formatting differences can cause your application to be rejected or require you to resubmit documents. Check that your name is written consistently on all your ID before applying.

## 2. NISA: Tax-Free Investment Account

### How It Works

NISA is Japan's tax-free investment account program. Any investment gains (dividends, capital gains from selling) earned within the account are exempt from the usual ~20% tax. The system was significantly reformed in 2024, and the new NISA has two investment allowances:

| Allowance | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| つみたて投資枠 | Designed for long-term, regular investing (index funds, etc.) with an annual contribution cap |
| 成長投資枠 | A broader allowance for individual stocks, ETFs, and more, with a higher annual cap |

Both allowances can be used simultaneously. There is a lifetime tax-exempt holding limit (check the latest official announcements for current figures).

### Key Things to Know

- **You can only hold one NISA account at one brokerage at a time**: you cannot have NISA accounts open at two different brokerages simultaneously
- If you want to transfer your NISA to another brokerage (転出), the process typically takes several weeks, during which you won't be able to open a new NISA

## 3. iDeCo: Individual Defined Contribution Pension

### How It Works

iDeCo is a self-directed retirement savings plan. Your monthly contributions (掛金) are **fully deductible from your taxable income**, reducing both income tax and 住民税 (residential tax) for the year. Investment gains within the account are also tax-free. Contribution limits vary depending on your employment type and whether you're enrolled in a corporate pension — check official sources for the current limits.

### Three Things Foreigners Need to Know

**① Your money is locked until age 60 (the biggest caveat)**

Funds in iDeCo are, in principle, **inaccessible until you turn 60**. Even if you leave Japan and return to your home country before then, the account stays locked — you cannot withdraw early. **If you're planning to leave Japan before 60, think carefully about whether locking up funds for the long term makes sense for you.**

**② You must actively transfer your account when changing jobs**

If you quit or change jobs and don't complete the iDeCo account transfer (移管) process within the required timeframe, your account can fall into a state where **management fees are being deducted but you can no longer make contributions or invest**. After leaving a job, confirm and handle the transfer as soon as possible.

**③ Rebalancing within iDeCo is tax-free**

Adjusting your asset allocation (rebalancing) within an iDeCo account has no tax implications — an advantage not available with regular brokerage accounts. Note that the results of rebalancing operations typically take 1–2 weeks to reflect.

## 4. Special Note for U.S. Citizens and Green Card Holders

U.S. citizens and permanent residents (green card holders) investing through NISA in Japan may face tax complications under U.S. law related to **PFIC (Passive Foreign Investment Company)** rules, which can limit the practical tax benefits of NISA. **We strongly recommend consulting a professional tax advisor before opening an account** to understand your options.

---

## 5. Other Useful Tips

- Opening a brokerage account through a cashback/points site (ポイントサイト) sometimes earns you more rewards than applying directly through the broker's website
- Rebalancing within iDeCo is tax-free, meaning you can periodically adjust your asset allocation without triggering additional tax liability
