# Credit Card Application Guide for Foreigners

In Japan, a credit card is more than just a payment tool — it's the key to building your personal credit history. For foreigners, getting that first card is often the hardest hurdle. But once you understand how the screening process works, your chances of approval improve dramatically.

---

## Why Your First Card Matters Most

Japan's credit bureaus (CIC/JICC) keep track of your credit history. Here's the catch: **having no record and having gone bankrupt look exactly the same in the system** — both show up as blank.

This means:
- The sooner you apply after arriving in Japan, the better your chances (student status is especially helpful)
- If you're 25 or older with no credit history at all, the system may suspect you've just been removed from a blacklist
- Once you have your first card and use it responsibly, getting additional cards becomes much easier in a chain reaction

## Application Process

1. **Prepare your documents**: 在留カード, bank account, phone number, proof of address
2. **Choose the right card type**: Retail and online-based cards tend to have more lenient screening than bank-issued cards
3. **Apply online or in person**: Some card types have higher approval rates when applied for in person
4. **Wait for screening**: Usually 1–2 weeks; some cards can be issued the same day

## Tips to Improve Your Approval Odds

- **Set your cash advance limit to ¥0**: This lowers your risk score — many people who were previously rejected got approved after making this change
- **Don't apply to multiple cards in a short period**: Applications are recorded for 6 months, and a string of rejections can create a vicious cycle
- **Be patient during your first year**: Not having a prior year's tax record is a common reason for rejection
- **Watch how you write your name**: Make sure your name is exactly the same on your bank account, 在留カード, and application form

## What to Do If You're Rejected

Rejection doesn't mean you can never get a card. Here's what to do:

1. **Wait at least 6 months** before applying to the same company again
2. In the meantime, try other issuers (you can apply to different companies consecutively)
3. Consider a **secured credit card** (requires a deposit but has almost no screening requirements)
4. Use a bank debit card to build a spending record and establish a sense of "being settled in"

## Credit-Building Roadmap

1. Arrive in Japan → Open a bank account immediately and get a debit card
2. Use the debit card for phone bills, online shopping, etc. to build a spending record
3. After 3–6 months → Apply for a card with relatively lenient screening
4. Once approved, use it responsibly → Apply for a second card after six months
5. As your credit history builds → Even premium bank-issued cards will be within reach
