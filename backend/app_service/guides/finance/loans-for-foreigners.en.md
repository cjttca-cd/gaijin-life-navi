# Loan Guide for Foreign Residents in Japan

Getting a loan in Japan — whether a home loan (住宅ローン) or a car loan — is far from impossible for foreign residents. Whether or not you have permanent residency (永住資格) is the biggest deciding factor, but even without it, there are viable paths if you understand how lenders evaluate applications and prepare accordingly.

---

## 1. Permanent Residency and Loan Eligibility

**If you have permanent residency**, lenders assess your application much the same way they would for a Japanese national, and you can apply for loan products at the vast majority of financial institutions.

**Without permanent residency:**

- Some financial institutions offer loan options for non-permanent residents, though the conditions tend to be stricter
- **Having already applied for permanent residency** (even while still under review) may relax the criteria at some institutions — this is often overlooked
- Major banks tend to have higher thresholds; smaller regional banks and online banks are often more flexible for non-permanent residents. It's common practice to try a different type of institution after being rejected by a major bank

## 2. Home Loans (住宅ローン) — Key Points

### What Lenders Look At

- **Employment stability**: The longer you've been with the same employer, the better your chances
- **Number of overseas dependents**: A high number of overseas family members may be cited as a reason for rejection at some major banks
- **Source of funds**: Lenders typically scrutinize the last **6 months of bank statements** closely — large deposits of unclear origin are best kept out of the statement you submit

### Important Notes on Down Payments (頭金)

- **Funds transferred from overseas are generally not accepted as a down payment**
- However, the same funds can typically be used for **early repayment (繰上返済)** — an important distinction that's easy to miss
- Deposits in Japanese bank accounts with a clear source are much more likely to be accepted

### Documents You'll Need (General Reference)

- 在留カード (Residence Card)
- Passport
- 収入証明 (Proof of income — most recent 確定申告書 or 源泉徴収票)
- Bank statements for the past 6 months
- Documents related to the property you're purchasing
- ※ Some institutions have asked for **Japanese language proficiency documentation**; check directly with your lender for their specific requirements

### Strategies to Improve Your Approval Chances

1. **Interest rates (金利) are negotiable**: Get quotes from multiple lenders, then use them as leverage in negotiations — there are real cases where this has successfully lowered rates. A quote from another institution (他行見積もり) is a powerful bargaining tool
2. **Joint guarantor (連帯保証人)**: If your spouse is a Japanese national, having them as a joint guarantor can significantly improve your approval chances
3. **Try a different type of institution**: Being rejected by a major bank doesn't mean it's over. Regional banks and online banks have more flexible criteria, and success stories are quite common

---

## 3. Car Loans (マイカーローン)

- **Financing arranged through dealerships typically comes with significantly higher interest rates than bank loans**; if possible, it's worth applying directly to a bank for a car loan first
- **New cars are easier to get approved for than used cars**: Manufacturer-affiliated finance companies tend to favor new car sales and have relatively lenient screening
- Foreign brand dealerships are generally more proactive about financing approvals than Japanese brands
- Among foreign residents without permanent residency, paying **in full with cash** is quite common — if loan approval is difficult, this is a realistic fallback option

## 4. The Importance of Credit History

Loan screening in Japan relies heavily on records from credit bureaus like **CIC (信用情報機関)**. Using a credit card regularly and paying on time over the long term is the most effective way to build a good credit history, and this directly and positively affects home loan screenings.

A thin or poor credit history can significantly reduce your chances of loan approval. From a loan application perspective, it's important to start building your credit record as early as possible.
