# Bank Account Opening Guide for Foreigners

A bank account is the foundation of daily life in Japan — you'll need one for receiving your salary, paying rent, utilities, and setting up a phone contract. The sooner you open one, the smoother everything else becomes. This guide covers the documents you'll need, your options for opening an account, and the common pitfalls to avoid.

---

## 1. Required Documents

You'll typically need the following when opening an account:

- **在留カード** (Residence Card): Required — must be valid
- **パスポート** (Passport): Some banks may ask to see it, but it's **not always required**. Your 在留カード is the primary ID document
- **印鑑 (personal seal) or signature**: Depends on the financial institution (see below)
- **マイナンバーカード** (My Number Card): Used for identity verification at some online applications; generally not required at branch counters

> ⚠️ **Common misconception about マイナンバー**: Many people think you need to provide your マイナンバー (Individual Number) to open a bank account — but that's not the case. **You don't need to submit your マイナンバー just to open an account.** It IS required for things like: **overseas remittances, opening a NISA account, or linking to マイナポイント**.
>
> Also, the **通知カード (notification card) was abolished in 2020** and is no longer accepted as an ID document at any financial institution. Make sure you have the photo ID version — the マイナンバーカード — not the old notification card.

## 2. Types of Banks

Japanese financial institutions fall into several broad categories, each with their own strengths:

| Type | Characteristics |
|------|-----------------|
| **Major Banks (City Banks)** | Nationwide branches, full-service; some have foreign language support |
| **Japan Post Bank** | Available at post offices across Japan; relatively accessible for newcomers |
| **Online Banks** | Fully digital, convenient process; usually requires a Japanese phone number and smartphone |
| **Foreign & Specialty Banks** | Better multilingual support; often more flexible with account opening criteria |

> Requirements for foreigners (minimum residency period, overseas remittance support, etc.) vary by institution.

## 3. How to Open an Account

### In-Branch Application (Counter)

Visit a branch in person, and a staff member will assist you.

- Some institutions allow you to open an account without a Japanese phone number
- 通帳 (passbook): Usually issued the same day; sometimes mailed to foreigners at a later date
- キャッシュカード (debit/ATM card): Always mailed — expect it in about **1–2 weeks**

### Online / App Application

Apply through the institution's official app or website.

- Usually requires a Japanese phone number that can receive **SMS verification**
- Your 在留カード can be verified using your smartphone's **NFC function**
- Your 口座番号 (account number) is typically issued within the same day to a few days; your キャッシュカード arrives in about 1–2 weeks by mail

> ⚠️ **Restrictions for those who have been in Japan less than 6 months**: Many financial institutions — especially online banks — restrict applications from foreigners who have been in Japan for less than 6 months. In-branch applications may be exceptions at some institutions. Conditions vary by bank and application method.

---

## 4. Personal Seal vs. Signature

**In-branch applications**: Some institutions require a personal seal (印鑑). Foreigners can have one made in katakana, romaji, or kanji (available at 100-yen shops or stamp stores).

**Online applications**: A digital signature is typically used instead — no 印鑑 needed.

---

## 5. Key Points to Remember

### Managing Your 在留カード Expiry

**An expired 在留カード can result in your account being frozen** — even while your renewal is in progress, so be careful.

- Start the renewal process at least **2 months before** your card expires
- After renewing, update your 在留カード information at the bank as soon as possible

### Overseas Remittance and Payment Apps

International remittance and payment apps usually **require a linked Japanese bank account** to work. Get your Japanese bank account set up first, then register for those services.

### After Opening Your Account

Once your キャッシュカード arrives, complete these initial steps:

- Activate your account and set a password via online banking or the app
- If you plan to send money overseas, you'll need to apply separately for that service (this usually requires your マイナンバー)
