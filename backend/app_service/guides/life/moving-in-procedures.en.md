# Administrative Procedures After Moving In

After moving to a new address, it's easy to get caught up in unpacking and forget about the official paperwork. In Japan, the **転入届** (move-in registration) is the foundation for all subsequent procedures — without it, you won't be able to obtain your 住民票 (Certificate of Residence), which can block address changes at banks, document delivery, and more. Getting this done early is key.

---

## Order of Priority

### 1. 転入届 — Top Priority

Head to the 役所 (city/ward office) in your new area and submit your 転入届. This is the **starting point for everything else** — take care of it first.

> ⚠️ **Moving between different municipalities:** You must first obtain a 転出届 (move-out certificate) from your old 役所, then bring it to your new 役所 to submit the 転入届. No 転出届 = no 転入届. You can submit the 転出届 online via **マイナポータル**, saving you a trip to your old 役所.

> ⚠️ **Moving within the same municipality** (within the same city or ward): You only need to submit a **転居届** — no 転出届 required, and no 転入届 either. Many people don't realize this and make an unnecessary trip to their old 役所.

### 2. 国民健康保険 Switch

When you submit your 転入届, you can usually handle the 国民健康保険 (National Health Insurance) address change or re-enrollment at the same counter on the same day — take care of it all at once.

### 3. マイナンバーカード Address Update

If you have a マイナンバーカード, you must update the address on it **on the same day** you submit your 転入届. Important: **if you don't do this within 90 days, the card will automatically become invalid.** Bring your card when you go to the 役所.

### 4. 在留カード Address Update

When foreign residents submit their 転入届, the 役所 will **automatically record the new address on the back of your 在留カード** — no separate application needed.

### 5. Other Procedures

- **Notify your employer**: Your address affects 社会保険 and 住民税, so let your company know
- **Driver's license address update**: Visit a 警察署 or 運転免許センター
- **郵便転送届 (mail forwarding request)**: Can be submitted **before** you move

## Easy-to-Miss Details

### 転出届 Can Be Submitted Online

When moving between municipalities, you normally need to visit your old 役所 in person to submit the 転出届. However, you can submit it online via **マイナポータル** (the government's online service portal), saving you the trip. **The 転入届 still needs to be done in person at your new 役所.**

### 国民年金 Address Update

- **Company employees (enrolled in 厚生年金)**: Your company handles this automatically — no action needed on your part
- **Self-employed or unemployed**: You'll need to submit the change yourself at the 役所

### 児童手当 (Child Allowance)

Families receiving 児童手当 need to complete both of these steps:
- At your old 役所: submit a 「受給事由消滅届」
- At your new 役所: submit a 「認定請求書」
- **Both must be completed within 15 days of moving**, or your payments may be interrupted

### 印鑑登録 Is Automatically Cancelled

Your 印鑑登録 (seal registration) from your old address is **automatically cancelled** when you submit your 転出届. If you still need to use your 印鑑 at your new address, you'll need to re-register at your new 役所.

## Tips for Getting It All Done Efficiently

You can save a lot of time by handling all of the following at your 役所 in a single visit:

- 転入届 ＋ 国民健康保険 ＋ 国民年金 (if applicable) ＋ マイナンバーカード address update ＋ 印鑑登録 (if needed)

**Quieter times at the counter**: Tuesday through Thursday mornings. Mondays, Fridays, and the start of the month tend to be busier — best to avoid those if you can.
