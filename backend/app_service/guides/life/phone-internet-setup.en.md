# Mobile & Internet Setup Guide for Foreign Residents in Japan

Getting a phone and internet connection is one of your top priorities when you first arrive in Japan. LINE registration, bank account SMS verification, identity confirmation for various services — many important procedures in Japan require a local phone number. This guide walks you through the process of setting up mobile and internet services as a foreign resident, along with key things to watch out for.

---

## 1. The Phone-and-Bank "Chicken-and-Egg" Problem

Many foreign residents run into this structural problem shortly after arriving:

- Most mobile carriers → require a credit card or Japanese bank account to sign a contract
- Most banks → online account opening requires SMS verification (which needs a Japanese phone number)

The result: you can't open a bank account without a phone, and you can't get a phone contract without a bank account.

**Choose your way out based on your situation:**

| Your Situation | Solution |
|--------|---------|
| Have an overseas credit card | Some carriers accept foreign credit cards — you can apply online and activate an eSIM the same day |
| Need voice calls, no credit card/account | SIM services for foreigners (accept コンビニ payments; some let you apply before arrival and pick up at the airport, so you're connected from day one) |
| Just need SMS verification, no credit card/account | Prepaid SMS-only SIM (no identity verification required, available on online shopping platforms; SMS-only, no voice calls) |

## 2. Documents Required for Signing Up

- **在留カード (Residence Card)**: Required (some carriers don't accept マイナンバーカード alone)
- **Passport**: Usually required alongside the 在留カード
- **Payment method**: Credit card, Japanese bank account, or コンビニ payment (varies by carrier)

> ⚠️ If your 在留カード shows less than one year of remaining validity, some carriers may apply stricter screening.

---

## 3. SIM Types and Activation Timelines

Under the revised Telecommunications Business Act (電気通信事業法, 2019), mobile devices and SIM contracts must be sold separately. You can insert a SIM directly into your existing phone (some phones may need to be SIM-unlocked first).

**eSIM (digital SIM)**: Can be activated **the same day** you sign up — no waiting for a physical card to arrive in the mail. ⚠️ Some carriers process applications submitted after 8 PM the following day.

**Physical SIM**: Mailed to you after sign-up, typically arriving in **a few days**, then activated by you.

**Ways to sign up:**
- **In-store** (家電量販店, etc.): Review the contract in person, get the card or activate eSIM same-day; some stores offer service in foreign languages
- **Online**: Flexible and convenient, but you'll generally need to handle issues on your own

---

## 4. Things to Watch Out For

- **Check your number type**: Make sure you get a number starting with 080 / 090 / 070. Numbers starting with 050 (VoIP) often can't receive SMS verification codes from banks and other services, and LINE registration may also fail
- **Decline unwanted add-ons**: You may be offered extra services when signing up (device insurance, security packages, etc.) — just say you don't need them
- **Returning your old SIM**: When switching or canceling a contract, some carriers require you to return the old SIM. Failing to do so may incur a cancellation fee — check in advance

---

## 5. Home Internet (光回線)

- **Building already has 光回線 equipment**: Service can usually be activated within 1–2 weeks of application, with no construction needed
- **Building doesn't have equipment**: Construction is required, typically taking **1–3 months**. **You'll generally need your landlord's permission first** (check your lease agreement)
- **While waiting for activation**: You can rent a ポケットWiFi — usually available from as short as one month
- **Watch out for early termination**: 光回線 contracts typically have a minimum term; canceling early may result in a fee

💬 *The 光回線 setup process varies depending on what equipment your building already has. The AI guide can give more specific advice based on your situation.*

---

## 6. Recommended Action Order

1. **Days 1–3 after arrival** — Visit your 役所 to complete 住民登録 and get your 住民票
2. **At the same time** — Sign up for a SIM based on your situation; get a Japanese phone number as soon as possible
3. **Weeks 1–2** — Once you have a stable phone number, move on to opening a bank account (see the bank account guide)
4. **Months 1–2** — Once your address and account are stable, apply for 光回線 if needed
