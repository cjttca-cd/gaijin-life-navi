# Guide to Japan Post Office Services

郵便局 (ゆうびんきょく, the post office) is one of Japan's most widely accessible public services — you'll find one within walking distance almost anywhere in the country. For foreign residents, it's not just a place to send letters and packages; it also plays a key role in managing your mail after you move. This guide covers the everyday services you'll use most, along with the mail forwarding process you should sort out right after moving.

---

## Common 郵便局 Services

郵便局 offers a range of sending options to suit different needs:

- **普通郵便 / 速達（そくたつ）**: Standard letter and document delivery. 速達 is the express option — typically delivered the next day.
- **レターパック**: A flat-rate envelope service available in two sizes. You can drop it in a mailbox or submit it at the counter. Comes with a tracking number, making it great for documents and small items.
- **ゆうパック**: Domestic parcel delivery, priced by size and destination. You can schedule a pickup online.
- **国際郵便 / EMS（国際スピード郵便）**: For sending mail or packages overseas. EMS is fast and comes with real-time tracking — a popular choice for sending gifts or documents to family back home.

For specific rates and delivery times, check the 郵便局 counter or the official website.

## Your First Priority After Moving: 郵便転送 (Mail Forwarding)

After 引越し (moving), mail addressed to your old address will keep arriving there. The 郵便局's 「転送届（てんそうとどけ）」 service automatically forwards mail from your old address to your new one — it's one of the most useful things you can do as part of your moving checklist.

### How to Apply

**e転居（online application）— Recommended**

The 郵便局 official website has an e転居 online application portal. You can complete the whole process from home without visiting a counter. It's simple and works for most situations.

**In person at 郵便局**

Visit your nearest 郵便局 and fill out a 転送届 paper form. Bring a valid ID (such as your 在留カード) when you submit it.

### Three Things to Know

**① You can apply before you move**

e転居 lets you set a specific start date. We recommend submitting your application a few days before your move — that way, forwarding kicks in on moving day with no gaps, and you won't miss any mail due to processing delays.

**② Valid for 1 year, renewable**

転送届 is valid for one year. After that, mail will no longer be forwarded. If you still need it, simply reapply — there's no limit on renewals.

**③ Some mail cannot be forwarded**

The following types of mail are generally **not** automatically forwarded:

- Mail marked with 「転送不要（てんそうふよう）」 — official notices from 役所, and some bank letters, often carry this label
- Parcels such as ゆうパック

These items won't be redirected to your new address even with a forwarding request in place.

## Don't Rely on 転送届 Alone — Update Your Address Directly

転送届 is a handy tool for the transition period after a move, but it has both a time limit and coverage gaps. To avoid missing important notices, make it a priority to contact the following organizations directly after you move and update your registered address:

- 役所 (for マイナンバー-related notices, 住民票, etc.)
- Banks and credit card issuers
- Organizations related to 在留カード and パスポート renewal notices

Aim to check and update these within two weeks of moving, so you don't miss any important deadlines or documents due to mail delays. For the full picture of post-move administrative procedures, see the 「引越し手続き指南」.
