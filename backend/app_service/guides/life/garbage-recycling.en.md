# Guide to Garbage Sorting and Recycling in Japan

In Japan, sorting your garbage correctly is a basic rule of community life that everyone is expected to follow. Compared to many other countries, Japan's sorting requirements are quite detailed — and for newcomers, it can feel overwhelming at first. Putting out the wrong type of garbage, or putting it out on the wrong day, can result in your trash being rejected and may also cause friction with your neighbors.

Japan's garbage sorting system is designed to efficiently separate recyclable resources, reduce the amount of waste sent to incineration, and protect the natural environment. This system is strictly enforced in every community, and foreign residents have the same responsibilities as local residents. Following the rules is also an important way to integrate into community life and show respect for your neighbors.

One important thing to know: garbage sorting rules vary by municipality (自治体) across Japan — how garbage is categorized and which days it's collected can differ depending on where you live. After moving into a new home, make sure to check the specific rules for your area as early as possible.

---

## Main Categories of Garbage

Household garbage in Japan is generally divided into the following major categories:

| Category | Japanese Name | Main Contents |
|----------|--------------|---------------|
| Burnable garbage | 燃えるごみ | Kitchen scraps, tissues, fabric, and other combustible items |
| Non-burnable garbage | 燃えないごみ | Metal items, ceramics, glass, and other non-combustible items |
| Recyclable resources | 資源ごみ | Cardboard, PET bottles, empty cans, glass bottles, and other recyclables |
| Bulk waste | 粗大ごみ | Furniture, bicycles, home appliances, and other large items (requires advance booking) |

The table above shows common categories, but the specific classification method may vary by municipality. Always follow the rules for your local area.

## How to Dispose of 粗大ごみ (Bulk Waste)

Large items that don't fit in regular garbage bags — such as furniture, bed frames, and bicycles — are classified as 粗大ごみ. They cannot be put out with regular garbage and require a separate booking process.

**Steps to dispose of bulk waste:**

1. **Book a collection**: Contact your municipality's 粗大ごみ reception desk by phone to request a collection service
2. **Confirm the collection details**: Staff will let you know the collection date, designated location, and disposal fee
3. **Purchase and attach a disposal sticker (シール)**: Buy the designated 粗大ごみ sticker at a コンビニ (convenience store) or similar shop for the amount indicated, and stick it prominently on the item
4. **Place the item on the designated day**: On the scheduled collection day, place the item at the designated spot and wait for it to be collected

There are also private waste collection companies that offer home pickup services, but their fees are typically much higher than the municipality's 粗大ごみ service. Making use of your municipality's service is much more economical.

## Inquiring About Garbage Rules and Multilingual Support

Because the rules differ by location, it's a good idea to visit your local **役所** (city/ward office) counter soon after moving in to confirm the garbage sorting rules. Note that phone inquiries are typically only available in Japanese — **visiting the counter in person** makes it much easier to access multilingual support.

City offices in major cities usually offer **multilingual services**:

- **Main supported languages**: English, Chinese, Korean, and others
- **Other languages**: Usually supported via tablet interpretation services
- **Easy Japanese**: Some offices have a "やさしい日本語" (easy Japanese) counter, which you can try even with limited Japanese ability
