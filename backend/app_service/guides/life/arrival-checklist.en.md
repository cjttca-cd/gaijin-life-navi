# Japan Arrival Checklist

There's one thing you must do within **14 days** of arriving in Japan — register your residency (住民登録). This is the foundation for everything else: without it, you can't open a bank account, sign a phone contract, get health insurance, or complete almost any other essential daily-life procedure.

This guide walks you through the most important initial steps after arriving in Japan, so you don't miss any critical deadlines.

---

## Who needs to do this?

**Mid- to long-term residents** (residence period over 3 months) are required to complete 住民登録.

This does not apply to:
- 短期滞在 (short-stay visas such as tourist visas)
- Residence periods of 3 months or less

---

## Step 1: Register Your Residency (転入届)

### Deadline
**Within 14 days of establishing your address**

This is a legal deadline. Missing it may cause problems with subsequent procedures, so make sure to complete it on time.

### Where to go
Your local **役所** (住民課 counter)

### What to bring
1. **在留カード** (Residence Card)
   - If you didn't receive your 在留カード at the airport, bring your passport (it will have a stamp saying "在留カード後日交付")
2. **Passport** (パスポート)
3. If you're moving in with family already living in Japan: **proof of family relationship** (birth certificate, marriage certificate, etc. — originals + Japanese translation)

### How it works
1. Go to the 住民課 counter at your local 役所
2. Fill out the 転入届
3. Submit your documents
4. Staff will write your address on the back of your 在留カード
5. Done!

💬 *Foreign language services at the counter vary by area. Your AI guide can tell you what's available locally.*

## Step 2: Get Your マイナンバー (Individual Number)

### What is マイナンバー?
It's Japan's 12-digit social security and tax number, assigned to every resident. Note: this is a **different number** from the 12-digit number on your 在留カード.

### How to get it
After completing 住民登録, the 役所 will automatically assign you a マイナンバー.

A few weeks later, you'll receive a letter called「**個人番号通知書**」in the mail with your マイナンバー. Important: **the 個人番号通知書 is just a notification — it cannot be used as proof of identity or as proof of your マイナンバー**. For that, you'll need your マイナンバーカード or a 住民票 (with マイナンバー included).

### マイナンバーカード (Strongly recommended — apply early)
The マイナンバーカード is a physical card with an IC chip. It lets you:
- Use it as official photo ID (one of the most important IDs alongside your 在留カード)
- Use it as your health insurance card (マイナ保険証 — traditional insurance cards were abolished in December 2024)
- Apply online for residence period renewals and other procedures
- Print your 住民票 and other documents at convenience stores

⚠️ **From June 2026, your 在留カード and マイナンバーカード can be combined** into a single card (「特定在留カード」system). Once combined, you won't need to visit the 役所 separately after updating your status at immigration. While it's not mandatory, it's a big convenience upgrade for foreign residents — **applying for your マイナンバーカード early is highly recommended**.

How to apply: by mail or online (via smartphone or computer).

## Step 3: Other Important Procedures

After completing 住民登録, you may also need to handle the following, depending on your situation:

| Procedure | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **国民健康保険** | If your employer doesn't provide social insurance, you'll need to enroll in 国民健康保険 |
| **国民年金** | Residents aged 20 and over are required to enroll |
| **Bank account** | See the bank account opening guide |
| **Phone contract** | See the mobile phone guide |

💬 *Whether you need these and in what order depends on your visa type and situation. Your AI guide can help you prioritize.*

## Timeline Summary

| When | What |
|------|------|
| Day of arrival | Pick up your 在留カード at the airport (available at major airports) |
| Within 14 days of establishing address | ⚠️ **Complete 住民登録 — required** |
| A few weeks after 住民登録 | Receive your「個人番号通知書」(number notification only — not valid as ID) |
| After that | Handle bank account, phone, insurance, and other follow-up procedures |
