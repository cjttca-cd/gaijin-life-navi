# Overview of Japan Residence Status (Visa) Types

In Japan, a foreigner's 在留資格 (commonly called a "visa" or "ビザ") determines what activities you can engage in, how long you can stay, and what path you can take toward permanent residency. Understanding which 在留資格 you hold — and how to maintain or upgrade it — is the very first step to life in Japan.

---

## Work-Type Residence Statuses

### 技術・人文知識・国際業務（技人国）

Designed for professionals in fields like IT engineering, accounting, translation, and language instruction. Generally requires a bachelor's degree or higher, or at least 10 years of relevant work experience. This is the most commonly held work visa category among foreign residents in Japan. **Up to 5 years.**

See the 技人国 Visa Renewal & Change Guide for more details.

---

### 高度専門職（Types 1-イ, 1-ロ, 1-ハ, and 2）

Japan's preferential visa for highly skilled professionals, using a **point-based system** (combining education, career history, annual income, age, and other factors). A score of 70 or above qualifies you to apply. Key benefits include a broader range of permitted activities, the ability to combine multiple work activities, and significantly shortened timelines for permanent residency applications (80+ points → as little as 1 year; 70+ points → as little as 3 years).

- **1号イ**: Academic research, professorships, and education
- **1号ロ**: Technology and IT
- **1号ハ**: Business management
- **2号**: Greatly relaxed activity restrictions, with no specific cap on the period of stay

See the 高度専門職 Point System Guide for more details.

---

### 経営・管理

For those running or managing a business in Japan, including self-employed entrepreneurs. Applicants typically need to demonstrate the substance of their business through capital investment or hiring. This visa undergoes strict scrutiny, with each renewal checking that the business is genuinely operating — even consecutive years of losses can lead to a denial. **Up to 5 years.**

> ⚠️ The 経営・管理 visa is among the most rigorously reviewed of all work visas. Providing false business plans will permanently and negatively affect all future visa applications.

---

### 技能

For foreign nationals with specialized skills — such as ethnic cuisine chefs, gem artisans, and stonemasons. Generally requires 10 or more years of practical experience (requirements vary by occupation). **Up to 5 years.**

---

### 特定技能（Types 1 and 2）

Created in 2019 to address labor shortages in specific industries including nursing care, construction, food service, accommodation, and manufacturing.

- **1号**: Covers 12 designated fields; requires passing a skills test and Japanese language test (or completion of 技能実習 Level 2). Maximum stay of 5 years, no family members permitted.
- **2号**: Open in certain fields; allows unlimited renewals, effectively creating a path close to permanent residency, and permits accompanying family members.

See the 特定技能 Visa Guide for more details.

---

### 技能実習（Types 1, 2, and 3）

Originally established to transfer technical skills to developing countries. Progression: Level 1 (up to 1 year) → Level 2 (up to 2 years) → Level 3 (up to 2 years), for a combined maximum of **5 years**. The gap between the system's stated purpose and its actual use has long been criticized, and the Japanese government has decided to reform it into a new 育成就労 system, which will be implemented gradually.

---

### 教授・研究・教育

Three residence statuses for academic activities:

- **教授**: Teaching and research at universities and other higher education institutions
- **研究**: Investigative and research work at research institutions
- **教育**: Teaching at elementary, middle, and high schools

The permitted scope of activities differs across these categories. **Up to 5 years.**

---

## Non-Work Residence Statuses

### 留学

For students enrolled at universities, 専門学校, Japanese language schools, and similar institutions. With a 資格外活動許可, you can work part-time for up to 28 hours per week (with an exception during long school breaks). **Up to 4 years and 3 months.**

See the 留学 Visa & 資格外活動 Guide for more details.

---

### 家族滞在

For spouses and minor children of holders of primary residence statuses such as work visas or 高度専門職. Full-time employment is generally not allowed; part-time work of up to 28 hours per week is permitted with a 資格外活動許可. Exceeding this limit can affect both the family member's and the primary visa holder's status. **Up to 5 years.**

💬 *The 家族滞在 visa has significant work restrictions. If you're looking to switch to a visa that allows full-time employment, the AI Navigator can help identify the right status change path for you.*

---

### 日本人の配偶者等

For foreign spouses of Japanese nationals, or those born as children of Japanese nationals. No restrictions on employment. **Up to 5 years.**

> Note: After a divorce or the death of your spouse, you must change to a different 在留資格 within 6 months, and report the change in marital status to the relevant authorities within 14 days.

See the 配偶者ビザ Guide for more details.

---

### 永住者の配偶者等

For spouses of 永住者, or those born in Japan as children of 永住者. No restrictions on employment. **Up to 5 years.**

---

### 特定活動

For specific activities not covered by other categories, as defined by ministerial notification. Key examples:

- **ワーキングホリデー**: For young people from partner countries (Australia, UK, Canada, etc.) — work and travel, **up to 1 year**
- **就職活動**: For graduates of universities, graduate schools, and 専門学校 who are still job hunting; can be extended once for 6 months, for a total of **up to 12 months** (requires documented, ongoing job-search activity during your studies)
- Others: EPA 介護福祉士候補者, Taiwanese working holiday, etc.

---

## Long-Term Resident Statuses

### 永住者

**No restrictions** on period of stay or employment. Note: the 在留カード must be renewed every **7 years** (forgetting to renew means being in violation of the card-carrying requirement). Typically requires 10 or more years of lawful residence; holders of 高度専門職 with 80+ points may be eligible in as little as 1 year; spouses of Japanese nationals or 永住者 can apply after 3 years of marriage and 1 year of continuous residence. Full payment of taxes and social insurance is an absolute requirement.

> ⚠️ After obtaining 永住者 status, any overdue taxes or social insurance payments may constitute grounds for revocation (per the 2022 system revision). Permanent residency is not a "set it and forget it" status — absence from Japan for 1 year or more can result in loss of status. Be sure to obtain a re-entry permit in advance.

See the 永住許可 Application Guide for more details.

---

### 定住者

For those with humanitarian or historical ties to Japan, such as people of Japanese descent (日系人) and recognized refugees. No restrictions on employment. **Up to 5 years.**

---

## Other Categories (For Reference)

### 文化活動・宗教

For those staying in Japan to study traditional Japanese culture (martial arts, tea ceremony, etc.) or to engage in religious activities. Primarily for unpaid activities; employment is generally not permitted. **Up to 3 years.**

### A Note on 帰化 (Naturalization)

帰化 is not a 在留資格 — it is the process of applying for Japanese citizenship. **永住者** status lets you keep your foreign nationality while staying in Japan indefinitely; **帰化** grants Japanese citizenship but typically requires renouncing your original nationality, after which you are no longer subject to immigration law. These are two very different paths, and the right choice depends on your personal circumstances.
