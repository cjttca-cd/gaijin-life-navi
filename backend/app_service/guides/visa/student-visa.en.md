# Japan Student Visa (在留資格「留学」) Application Guide

Applying for a student visa is the first — and often trickiest — step to studying in Japan. The 在留資格「留学」 process has two stages: your school applies to 入管 (the Immigration Services Agency) for a **Certificate of Eligibility (COE / 在留資格認定証明書)** on your behalf, and then you bring that COE to a Japanese consulate or embassy in your home country to apply for the actual visa. The process itself isn't complicated, but small mistakes in the paperwork can lead to rejection or delay your enrollment.

---

## 1. Application Process Overview

1. **Submit your application to the school** → Receive your acceptance letter
2. **School applies to 入管 for the COE on your behalf** → Wait for approval (can take several weeks to months — plan ahead)
3. **Bring the COE to your nearest Japanese consulate to apply for the visa**
4. **Enter Japan after receiving your visa** — 在留カード (Residence Card) is issued by 入管 upon arrival

The whole process involves significant waiting periods, so **start early and give yourself plenty of buffer time** to avoid any delays affecting your enrollment date.

---

## 2. Proof of Finances — The Most Common Time Trap

This is one of the most frequent reasons for visa rejection — and it's almost entirely avoidable with a little planning.

**Japanese consulates require that your bank balance certificate be issued within 6 months of your visa application date.**

In real cases, applicants have been rejected simply because their certificate was issued more than 6 months before the application. Reapplying then required additional documents like a statement of fund sources and a guarantor's proof, making the process significantly more complicated.

**Tip**: Wait until you've confirmed your visa appointment date before going to the bank to obtain the certificate. Getting it too early can cause it to expire before you apply. The certificate typically requires an original with a bank seal plus transaction records covering the relevant period. Always check the latest requirements from your specific consulate.

## 3. Application Form Update (from March 2023)

**Starting March 17, 2023**, the Japan student visa application form was officially updated. Key differences from the old version:

- **Original COE not required**: You only need to submit a **copy** of the COE. Keep the original safe — you may need it when entering Japan.
- **New field added**: The 「在留資格認定証明書番号」 (COE number on the certificate) now needs to be filled in on the application form.

If you use the old form, the consulate may ask you to fill it out again. Before submitting, make sure you're using the **latest version** of the form.

---

## 4. Filling Out the Application Form

For the field **「予定在日滞留期間」** (planned duration of stay in Japan), it's recommended to write it in word form rather than as a number of days:

| Style | Example |
|-------|---------|
| ✅ Recommended (word form) | 「一年三か月」「二年三か月」 |
| ❌ Not recommended (days) | 「457日」「822日」 |

The word form is cleaner and more intuitive. Consulates accept this format too, and it avoids unnecessary confusion.

---

## 5. Timing Your 入管 Visit

When you need to handle procedures at 入管 inside Japan (such as picking up your 在留カード), keep these tips in mind:

- **Avoid busy seasons**: March–April (graduation/employment season) and September–October (fall enrollment season) are the most crowded times of year — wait times can be significantly longer
- **Best days to go**: Tuesday through Thursday are generally less crowded (Monday and Friday tend to be busier)
- **Online reservation system**: 入管 offers an online appointment system for certain procedures — not many people know about this, so check if your procedure can be booked in advance to skip the walk-in queue

## 6. During and After Your Studies

Getting the visa is just the beginning. During your time in Japan as a student, and after graduation, you'll typically need to deal with work permission (資格外活動許可) for part-time jobs, visa renewals, and changing your status to a work visa after graduation — all of which vary by individual situation.
