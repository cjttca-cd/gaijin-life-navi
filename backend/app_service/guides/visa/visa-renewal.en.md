# Residence Status Renewal Guide

Renewing your 在留期間 may feel routine, but it requires real preparation. Approval is not automatic — even long-term residents can face denial or end up with a shorter period than expected. Knowing the common pitfalls in advance is the key to getting through the process smoothly.

---

## When to Apply

**You can apply as early as 3 months before your expiry date.**

Don't wait until the last minute: if you receive a request for additional documents, or your application falls during a busy period, you might run out of time to respond.

### Busy Seasons

- **Most crowded**: March–April (graduation and job-start season) and September–October (autumn enrollment)
- Processing times can stretch significantly during these periods — there are real cases where March applications weren't decided until June
- **For in-person visits**: Tuesday–Thursday tend to be quieter; Monday and Friday are busier

---

## Common Document Pitfalls

Missing even one document will get your application **rejected on the spot**, meaning another trip. Common issues:

- **Name discrepancy between your 在留カード and company documents** (traditional vs. simplified characters, katakana spelling differences) → check every field carefully before you go
- A request for additional documents is not the same as a denial — there are cases where applications were approved within 2 weeks after submitting 9 extra items

---

## How to Apply

### ① In Person

Submit your documents at the **出入国在留管理局** that covers your area of residence.

Things to keep in mind:
- Bring a complete set of documents plus **photocopies** (you may be asked to provide extras on the spot)
- 収入印紙 (revenue stamps) can be purchased inside the office, but there may be a queue
- Wait times are typically **2–5 hours** — consider taking the whole day off
- Some procedures support **online reservations**, which can significantly cut wait times — many people aren't aware this option exists, so check ahead of time

### ② Online

- Available for: 在留期間 renewal, residence status changes, re-entry permits, and more
- **PC only**; requires a マイナンバーカード and an IC card reader
- All documents must be combined into a single PDF; file size limits apply
- Occasional system errors have been reported, but processing tends to be faster than in-person
- ⚠️ **You still need to visit 入管 in person to receive your new 在留カード** — the process cannot be completed entirely online

---

## Processing Times and 特例期間

- Processing times can extend significantly during busy periods
- If your 在留期間 expires while your application is still under review, you can continue to reside legally in Japan during the "**特例期間**"
- ⚠️ **Avoid traveling abroad during 特例期間**: leaving the country before receiving your result notification may prevent you from receiving it properly

---

## Reading Your Notification

The type of mail you receive can give you an early clue about the outcome:

| Mail Type | What It Means |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Postcard (はがき)** | Generally good news — a notice to come pick up your result |
| **Registered mail (書留)** | May indicate a request for additional documents or a denial — read it carefully |

If your application is denied, the reason won't be stated in writing — you'll need to visit 入管 in person to find out.

---

## Risk of a Shorter Renewal Period

Your renewed 在留期間 isn't guaranteed to stay the same or get longer — it can drop from 5 years down to 3 years, or even just 1 year. The risk is higher in these situations:

- Applying for renewal shortly after changing jobs
- Fixed-term employment (contract worker)
- Decrease in income
- Late payments of taxes or social insurance premiums

**Note**: Consistently receiving 1-year periods may affect the residency period requirements for permanent residency (永住) applications — plan ahead if this applies to you.

## Job Changes and Renewal

- Going **more than 3 months without employment** after changing jobs significantly raises the risk of denial
- After a job change, consider applying for a **就労資格証明書** — it can help make your next renewal go more smoothly

---

## About 行政書士 (Application Agents)

Consider using a licensed **申請取次行政書士** (who can submit applications on your behalf without you visiting 入管) in these situations:

- You can't take a full weekday off
- You've had a previous denial
- You have difficulty communicating in Japanese
- Your situation is complex (e.g., business management visa)

For straightforward renewals (same company, same residence status), self-filing and using an agent generally produce similar outcomes.
