# Complete Guide to Changing Your Residence Status in Japan

When your life situation or work activities change significantly in Japan, you'll often need to update your 在留資格 (residence status) to match. Continuing activities that don't align with your current status can lead to renewal refusals — or even being required to leave the country. Understanding the process for changing your status is an essential part of living in Japan.

---

## 1. When Do You Need to Change Your 在留資格?

Here are the most common situations that require a change:

- **留学 → Work**: After graduating and starting a job, you'll need to change from 「留学」 to a work-related status like 「技術・人文知識・国際業務」
- **家族滞在 → Work**: The 「家族滞在」 status only allows part-time work up to 28 hours per week with a 資格外活動許可. For full-time employment, you'll need to switch to a work status
- **技能実習 → 特定技能**: If you want to keep working in Japan after your 技能実習 period ends
- **Marriage**: Spouses of Japanese nationals or permanent residents can apply for 「日本人の配偶者等」 or a similar status

**Switching jobs within the same status doesn't require a change**: For example, if you move to a new role that still falls under 「技術・人文知識・国際業務」, your status itself stays the same — but you must file a 「所属機関変更届出」 with 入管 within **14 days**. If your new role involves substantially different duties, it's worth getting a 「就労資格証明書」 to confirm you're still covered.

---

## 2. Application Process

1. **Check the requirements for your target status**: Review the education, job category, and duties requirements to make sure you qualify
2. **Gather your documents**: What you'll need depends on your situation — call 入管 before submitting to confirm if anything extra is required
3. **Submit your application at the 入管 office that covers your address**
4. **Wait for a decision**: Processing typically takes 1–3 months
5. **Pick up your new 在留カード**: When approved, bring your notification letter, passport, and 在留カード to the 入管 window to collect it
6. **Application fee**: ¥4,000 (paid via 収入印紙, available at convenience stores — confirm the amount at time of application)

## 3. Required Documents (Example: 留学 → 技人国)

**Provided by the applicant:**
- 在留資格変更許可申請書 (applicant section — first 2 pages)
- Passport (original)
- 在留カード
- Photo (40×30mm)
- Degree certificate or 卒業見込証明書 (expected graduation certificate)
- Resume / CV

**Provided by the employer:**
- 在留資格変更許可申請書 (company section — last 5 pages)
- Copy of employment contract
- 法定調書合計表 (legal withholding summary)
- Company registration certificate (登記簿謄本)
- Financial statements
- Company profile / brochure

※ This is a typical document list. Your specific situation may require additional materials. Always confirm with 入管 before submitting.

---

## 4. Important Things to Know

**① Tourist visas (短期滞在) generally can't be changed**
Applications to change from a 「短期滞在」 (tourist/short-stay) status to another residence status are generally not accepted while you're in Japan.

**② Activity restrictions during the application period**
After you submit your application and before a decision is made, you can only engage in activities permitted under your **current** 在留資格. For example, if you're applying to change from 留学 to 技人国, you cannot start full-time work during this waiting period.

**③ What to do if your application is denied**
If your application is rejected, you'll typically be given 31 days to prepare to leave Japan. During this period, you can try applying for a different status, but the chances of success are low. It's best to consult a 行政書士 or other professional as early as possible.

**④ Common reasons applications fail**
- Mismatch between your 在留資格 and your actual activities
- Failure to file required notifications (e.g., not reporting a change of address or employer)
- Accumulated violations or legal issues

---

## 5. Special Cases

**New graduates still job hunting**: If you've graduated but haven't found a job yet, you can apply to change to 「特定活動」 (job-seeking). This is valid for 6 months and can be extended once, for a maximum of 12 months. You'll need to provide evidence that you've been actively job hunting since before graduation.

**Working after graduating from a language school**: 「技術・人文知識・国際業務」 generally requires a university degree or equivalent experience (10+ years in the relevant field). Language school graduation alone typically doesn't qualify. If you want to work after graduating from a language school, you'll need to explore other status options.
