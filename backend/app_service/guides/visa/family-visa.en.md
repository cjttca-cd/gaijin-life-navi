# Practical Guide to Family Visa (家族滞在) Applications in Japan

If you're a foreign national living in Japan on a work or student visa, you can apply to bring your spouse and minor children to Japan under the 家族滞在 residence status. This is the key visa category for family reunification — but immigration procedures can take longer than you'd expect, so knowing what to prepare in advance makes a big difference.

---

## What is 家族滞在?

家族滞在 is a 在留資格 (residence status) specifically for the spouses and minor children of foreign nationals who hold certain visa types in Japan (work, study, etc.). Once approved, family members can legally live in Japan and may apply for **資格外活動許可** (permission for activities outside permitted scope) to work part-time within set limits.

Note: If your spouse is a Japanese national, the applicable status is 「日本人の配偶者等」, which is a separate 在留資格 from 家族滞在.

## Before You Apply: What to Expect at 入管

Both new applications and renewals for 家族滞在 are handled in person at the **出入国在留管理局（入管）**. Wait times are notoriously long, so planning ahead is essential.

### Wait Times and Best Times to Visit

- Expect to wait **2–5 hours** at the counter — plan for a full day
- **Arrive 30–60 minutes before opening** (doors open at 9:00) to get in earlier
- Best days: **Tuesday through Thursday** tend to be less crowded; Mondays and Fridays are busier
- Busiest periods of the year: **March–April (graduation/job-start season)** and **September–October (school start)** — avoid if possible

### Online Reservation and Online Application

**Online reservation**: For certain procedures like picking up your 在留カード, you can skip the in-person queue by booking through the official reservation system, which supports Chinese and other languages. Many people don't know this exists — check before you go.

**オンライン申請 (online application)**: Eligible procedures can be submitted online via PC, and processing is generally faster than at the counter. However, you'll need a マイナンバーカード and an IC card reader, and you'll still need to visit 入管 in person to receive your 在留カード.

---

## Day-of Checklist and Tips

When you head to 入管, make sure you bring:

- Original documents + **copies of everything** (the officer may ask for extras)
- 収入印紙 (revenue stamps) — available inside 入管, but the line can be long, so grab them in advance if you can
- Charger, snacks, and something to keep you occupied (2–5 hour waits are normal)

A few things to keep in mind:

- **If documents are missing, you can't fix it on the spot** — you'll need to go home and come back
- Requirements can vary by officer, so prepare your documents thoroughly
- If you receive a request for **additional materials**, that's not a rejection — it means your application is moving forward

## Two Support Options When You're Unsure

### FRESC（外国人在留支援センター）

An official free consultation center in Yotsuya that supports **20 languages**. Visiting FRESC before your 入管 appointment to confirm which documents you need can save you a second trip.

### 申請取次行政書士

A 申請取次行政書士 can **submit your application to 入管 on your behalf** — no personal appearance required. This is especially worth considering if:

- You can't take time off work on weekdays
- You've had a previous application denied
- You're not confident in Japanese or your case is complex
