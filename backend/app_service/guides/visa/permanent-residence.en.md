# Permanent Residency Application Guide for Foreign Residents in Japan

Permanent residency (永住権) is one of the ultimate goals for foreign residents in Japan. Once obtained, you no longer need to renew your 在留資格 periodically, and you gain significantly more freedom in where you live and work. That said, the screening process is strict, and there are plenty of easy-to-miss pitfalls. This guide draws on real-world experience to walk you through the key points so you can prepare thoroughly before applying.

---

## Residence Status Requirements

At the time of application, your current 在留期間 must be **3 years or more**. Those holding a 1-year status have virtually no chance of approval in practice, and often get stuck in a cycle of only being able to renew for 1 year at a time — unable to move toward 永住申請. Additionally, you must have **lived in Japan for at least 9 out of the 12 months prior to applying** (入管 checks your entry and exit records).

---

## Tax and Social Insurance: The Biggest Hidden Risk

This is the most common reason applications are rejected — please take it seriously.

- **Even a single late payment on 住民税 can directly result in a rejection** — no matter how impressive your education, income, or employer
- Paying overdue amounts later **does not erase the record of late payment** — 入管 won't overlook it
- The practical requirement is "no late payments in the past 2 years"
- After changing jobs, if your withholding switches from 特別徴収 to 普通徴収, responsibility for payment falls on you — it's easy to forget and end up with a late payment record. Make sure to request a switch back to 特別徴収 at your new employer as soon as possible
- Your 年金 must be fully paid up for the past 2 years — setting up automatic bank transfers is strongly recommended

## Income Assessment: What to Watch Out For

- The annual income used in screening is **based on the figure on your 課税証明書**, not your employment contract or pay slips
- **Housing allowances and other benefits are not counted as income** (they're non-taxable and excluded from the income figure used in point calculations)

---

## Applying via the 高度専門職 Points System

If you're applying for 永住権 through the points-based 高度専門職 route, keep the following in mind:

- **70-point route**: You must demonstrate that you maintained 70+ points continuously for the past **3 years**
- **80-point route**: You must demonstrate that you maintained 80+ points continuously for the past **1 year**
- Points must meet the threshold both "at the time of application" and "throughout the required period"
- You do **not** need to have changed your 在留資格 to 高度専門職 — you can apply using the points system while holding 技術・人文知識・国際業務 or other statuses

## How Long Does Screening Take?

The official standard is about 4 months, but **in practice, the wait is typically 8 to 12 months**. During this time, 入管 may send 1 to 3 requests for additional documents, each adding roughly 1 to 2 months to the wait. After submitting your application, stay reachable and respond to any follow-up requests promptly.

---

## The Reason Statement (理由書)

The 理由書 is a free-form document, but it's best to address each of the three statutory requirements for 永住申請 separately: ① good conduct (素行善良), ② financial self-sufficiency, and ③ benefit to Japan's national interests. Including concrete commitments — such as homeownership or intent to settle long-term — along with recommendation letters from a supervisor or academic advisor, can strengthen your application considerably.

---

## If Your Application Is Rejected

Rejection notices follow a fixed format and do not include specific reasons. To find out why, you must visit 入管 **within 20 days of receiving the notice** and ask the reviewing officer in person. If you're not confident in your Japanese, bring someone who can communicate fluently on your behalf.

---

## After You Receive 永住権

永住権 is not something you can simply forget about once you have it. Keep these points in mind:

- **Your 在留カード must be renewed every 7 years** (永住者 cards have an expiry date too — carrying an expired card is illegal)
- **Residing outside Japan for more than 1 year may result in the loss of your 永住権** — if you plan to be away long-term, apply for a 再入国許可 in advance (valid for up to 5 years)
- **As of the 2022 legal revision**, failing to pay taxes or social insurance can now be grounds for revoking 永住権 — your obligations don't end once you receive it

---

## Should You Use a 行政書士?

If your documents are in order and you're comfortable with Japanese, applying on your own is entirely feasible. However, if you have a prior rejection on record, a complex work history, late tax payments, or difficulty communicating in Japanese, working with a 行政書士 is strongly recommended.
