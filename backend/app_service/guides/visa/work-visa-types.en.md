# Types of Work Visa (在留資格) in Japan Explained

To work for pay in Japan, you must hold a 在留資格 (residence status/visa) that matches the type of work you do. Each status has its own scope of permitted occupations, eligibility requirements, and maximum period of stay. It's common to discover that your current visa doesn't cover a new job when switching careers — so knowing the differences upfront is essential for working in Japan.

---

## Overview of Major Work Visas

### 技術・人文知識・国際業務 (技人国)

The most widely held work visa among foreign residents in Japan, commonly called "技人国." It covers three broad fields:

- **Technical (技術系):** IT engineers, system development, data analysis, etc.
- **Humanities/Social Sciences (人文知識系):** Accounting, marketing, legal affairs, etc.
- **International Services (国際業務系):** Translation, cross-cultural business support, etc.

Applicants generally need a university degree or at least 10 years of relevant work experience. Maximum stay is 5 years per period.

### 特定技能 1号・2号

A work visa for specific industries such as food service, construction, and nursing care (介護):

- **1号:** Requires passing a skills test and a Japanese language test; total stay capped at 5 years
- **2号:** Higher skill requirements; renewable indefinitely (eligible industries are gradually expanding)

### 経営・管理

For investors and managers running a business in Japan. Renewal requires proof of business performance, and the screening process is rigorous.

### 高度専門職

A points-based (ポイント制) visa for highly skilled professionals. Scoring 80 points or more allows you to apply for permanent residency (永住) after just one year — making it one of the fastest paths to 永住権.

### 技能

For foreign nationals with specific skilled trades (e.g., foreign cuisine chefs, construction specialists). Typically requires 10+ years of work experience in your home country.

## Application Practicalities: Online vs. 行政書士

### Online Application (オンライン申請)

Procedures like 在留資格変更 and 在留期間更新 can be submitted through the immigration bureau's online system. A few things to keep in mind:

- **PC only** — mobile devices are not supported
- Requires a マイナンバーカード and an IC card reader
- All documents must be combined into **a single PDF file**, with a file size limit
- The system occasionally has errors, but processing is generally faster than in-person
- **Picking up your 在留カード still requires an in-person visit** — the process cannot be completed entirely online

### 行政書士 (Authorized Representative)

A 行政書士 with 申請取次資格 can submit your application to immigration on your behalf — no need to visit in person.

**When it's worth hiring one:**
- Difficulty taking time off work to visit immigration on weekdays
- Previous application rejections on record
- Complex cases (e.g., first-time 経営管理 visa application)
- Difficulty communicating in Japanese

**When you might not need one:**
- Simple renewals with the same company and same 在留資格 — self-filing is generally just as effective

Also, if your company's HR department holds 申請取次 authorization, they can file on your behalf — usually at no charge.
