# Tax Return (確定申告) Guide for Foreign Residents in Japan

If you live in Japan, you may be required to file a 確定申告 — Japan's annual individual income tax return — each year. Even if your employer has already handled 年末調整, there are situations where you still need to file on your own. Understanding your filing obligations helps you avoid tax penalties and may even result in a refund of overpaid taxes.

---

## Who Needs to File?

You **generally need** to file a 確定申告 if you:

- Are an employee whose company **did not perform 年末調整**
- Have **income from two or more sources** (main job + side job, etc.)
- Earned more than ¥200,000 in side income or other miscellaneous income
- Are **self-employed** (sole proprietor, freelancer, contract worker, etc.)
- Have **interest income from overseas bank accounts** (which cannot be processed through 年末調整)
- Want to claim deductions not covered by 年末調整, such as **医療費控除**

> ⚠️ Even if your side income is under ¥200,000 and you don't need to file for income tax, **you may still be required to file for 住民税 (resident tax)**. These are separate obligations — don't confuse them.

## Filing Period

The 確定申告 filing window is generally **February 16 to March 15** each year (may be extended if it falls on a holiday).

> Check the 国税庁 (National Tax Agency) official announcements for the exact dates each year.

---

## Three Ways to File

### 1. e-Tax (Online Filing)

File from home at your own convenience. **Common issues for foreign residents — and how to fix them:**

- Set your browser's language preference to **Japanese first**, otherwise pages may appear blank
- If you run into errors on a PC, switching to the **MynaPortal app on your smartphone** is often more reliable
- The smartphone version supports **camera scanning of your 源泉徴収票** for automatic data entry
- If you have connection issues, try switching to IPv4 or using your mobile data

### 2. In-Person at the 税務署

During filing season, 税務署 staff will guide you through the smartphone-based filing process.

- It's strongly recommended to **book an appointment via LINE** in advance — walk-ins can mean hours of waiting
- If you don't have a compatible device, some 税務署 locations offer **tablet or smartphone lending**

### 3. Paper Filing (by Mail)

Download and fill out the paper tax return form, then mail it to your local 税務署.

## Special Notes for Foreign Residents

### Dependent Deduction for Overseas Family Members (海外扶養控除)

When claiming a deduction for overseas dependents, keep these rules in mind:

- **Overseas dependents aged 30–69** (rules tightened from 2023): must meet at least one of the following conditions — ① currently studying abroad, ② has a disability, or ③ you remit a certain amount to them each year. Remittances must be documented transaction by transaction — a single lump-sum transfer is not accepted
- **Overseas dependents aged 70+**: the applicable deduction category is「老人扶養親族（同居老親等以外）」— do not mistakenly select「同居老親等」, which carries a different (higher) deduction amount. Filing the wrong category may require an amended return

💬 *Eligibility for 扶養控除 depends on your specific family situation. The AI Guide can help you figure out what applies to you.*

### Foreign Insurance Does Not Qualify for 生命保険料控除

Life insurance purchased in your home country **does not qualify** for Japan's 生命保険料控除. Only policies underwritten by Japanese insurance companies are eligible.

### Tax Scope for Non-Permanent Residents (非永住者 / NPR)

Foreign residents who have lived in Japan for less than a certain number of years are classified as 非永住者 (NPR). The rules around taxation of overseas income are nuanced — **the common assumption that "overseas income is only taxed if remitted to Japan" has important exceptions. Don't rely on old assumptions when determining your filing obligations.**

💬 *Wondering whether your overseas income as a 非永住者 needs to be reported? The AI Guide can analyze your situation based on your years of residence and income structure.*

### Catching Up on Deductions Missed During 年末調整

If you missed claiming a deduction during 年末調整 (such as 医療費控除), you can still file a 還付申告 to claim a refund **within 5 years from the filing deadline**.
