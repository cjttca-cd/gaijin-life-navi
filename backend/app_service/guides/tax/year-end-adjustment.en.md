# Year-End Tax Adjustment Guide for Foreign Residents in Japan

If you work at a company in Japan, you go through a key tax process every year at year-end called **年末調整** (nen-matsu chōsei). Your employer handles the final settlement of your income tax for the year — refunding any overpayment or collecting any shortfall. Understanding the process and the specific considerations for foreign residents can help you avoid missing deductions and overpaying tax.

---

## What is 年末調整?

Every month, **源泉徴収** (withholding tax) is deducted from your salary based on estimated amounts. At year-end, your company compares the total withheld against your actual tax liability:

- Overpaid → refund reflected in your December or January paycheck
- Underpaid → the difference is collected from your paycheck

**Who it applies to:** Employees working at a single company who have submitted their 年末調整 declaration forms to their employer. Self-employed individuals and sole proprietors must file 確定申告 separately — 年末調整 does not apply to them.

---

## How the 年末調整 Process Works

| Timing | What Happens |
|--------|--------------|
| November–December | Submit 年末調整 declaration forms to your company |
| December | Company completes the income tax settlement; difference reflected in your paycheck |
| Following January | Receive your **源泉徴収票** (a statement of your annual income and tax withheld — also useful as proof of income) |
| Following Feb 16 – Mar 15 | File 確定申告 if required |

## Special Considerations for Foreign Residents

### 1. 扶養控除 for Overseas Dependents

If you have parents or other dependents living outside Japan, you may be able to claim the overseas 扶養控除. However, **rules for overseas relatives aged 30–69 were tightened starting in 2023** — they must meet one of the following conditions:

1. Studying in Japan as an international student (proof of enrollment required)
2. Holding a disability certificate
3. Receiving **¥380,000 or more per year in remittances from you** — and each dependent must receive their own separate transfer; combined transfers to multiple people don't count

**For overseas dependents aged 70 and older:** Note the classification carefully. Overseas parents fall under「老人扶養親族（同居老親等以外）」, not「同居老親等」. These categories have different deduction amounts, and a misclassification may require you to file an amended return later.

### 2. Overseas Insurance is Not Eligible for 生命保険料控除

Insurance policies purchased in your home country (even if you're still paying premiums) **do not** qualify for Japan's 生命保険料控除. Only policies with Japanese insurance companies can be claimed.

### 3. Income Threshold Changes

**Starting with the fiscal year 令和7 (2025)**, the basic deduction and the employment income deduction have both increased. The income threshold below which no income tax is owed has risen from ¥1.03 million to **¥1.23 million**. If you're claiming 配偶者控除 or 配偶者特別控除, make sure to check the updated income limits for those as well.

## Do You Still Need to File 確定申告 After 年末調整?

Even if your company has completed 年末調整, you'll still need to file 確定申告 between February 16 and March 15 of the following year if any of the following apply:

- Annual income exceeds ¥20 million
- Side income exceeds ¥200,000
- You receive salary from two or more employers
- You left your job mid-year and did not find another one (a refund is usually available)
- You're claiming the housing loan deduction (住宅ローン控除) for the first time (from the second year onward, this can be handled through 年末調整)
- You have 医療費控除, or ふるさと納税 donations to more than 5 municipalities

⚠️ **住民税 note for side income:** Even if your side income is under ¥200,000 (meaning no 確定申告 required for income tax), you still need to report it separately to your municipality for 住民税 purposes. If you do file 確定申告, the information is forwarded automatically — no separate submission needed.

**Deductions missed during 年末調整** can be recovered through a refund filing (還付申告) within 5 years. So don't worry if you missed the deadline for the current year.
