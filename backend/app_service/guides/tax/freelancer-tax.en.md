# Tax Guide for Freelancers in Japan

Working as a freelancer (フリーランス) or running a side business in Japan involves significantly more tax complexity than regular employment. From business registration to choosing your filing method to navigating the インボイス制度 — there are pitfalls at every step. This guide covers the key tax knowledge foreign residents need when starting self-employment or a side business in Japan.

---

## Step 1: File Your 開業届

To start a self-employed business in Japan, you need to submit a **開業届** (business registration notice) to your 税務署.

- You can submit it **online via e-Tax** — no need to visit the 税務署 in person. It's a notification system, so you won't receive any approval notice back.
- Filing the 開業届 itself **does not affect your residence status**. However, if your business activities fall outside the scope permitted by your visa, you'll need to separately apply for **資格外活動許可** (permission to engage in activities outside your visa status). Check with immigration authorities beforehand.

## 青色申告 vs 白色申告: Choosing Your Filing Method

Individual income tax filing in Japan comes in two forms:

| | 青色申告 | 白色申告 |
|--|---------|---------|
| Special deduction | Yes | No |
| Prior application required | Yes (submit approval application) | No |
| Bookkeeping requirement | Double-entry bookkeeping | Simple records |

青色申告 offers larger deductions and is generally more advantageous for self-employed individuals, but requires an advance application.

**Deadline to submit 青色申告承認申請書:**

- Within **2 months** of your business start date, or
- By **March 15** of the applicable tax year

⚠️ If you miss these deadlines, **you can only use 白色申告 for that year** — you cannot apply 青色申告 retroactively.

**Special note for side businesses:** Income from a side job alongside regular employment is typically classified as **雑所得 (miscellaneous income)** and is generally not recognized as 事業所得 (business income), which means 青色申告 may not be available. To qualify as 事業所得, you generally need annual sales of **¥3 million or more**, or be able to demonstrate continuous business operations with complete bookkeeping records.

## インボイス制度: Do You Need to Register for Consumption Tax?

Japan introduced the **インボイス制度** (Qualified Invoice System) in 2023. Whether you need to register mainly depends on your client type:

- **Corporate clients:** If you don't register as an インボイス-issuing business, clients may request price reductions or terminate contracts
- **Individual consumers:** Registration is generally not required
- A **2割特例** (transitional simplified calculation measure) is currently available — check the 税務署 or official website for the applicable period

## 確定申告: Key Points and Common Questions

### Reporting Foreign-Source Income

- Income received from overseas companies is generally **not subject to 源泉徴収 (withholding tax)** in Japan, so you must report the full amount in your 確定申告.
- Foreign currency income must be converted to yen. The 国税庁 specifies using the **TTS (Telegraphic Transfer Selling) rate**; monthly average exchange rates are also accepted in practice.

### Tips for Using e-Tax

- Set your browser language to **Japanese as the top priority** — otherwise pages may appear blank.
- The most reliable method is using a **smartphone + MynaPortal app**, which also supports automatic reading of 源泉票 by photo.
- When using MynaPortal's automatic linkage feature, if the romanized name on your マイナンバーカード doesn't match the katakana name on your insurance card, the linkage may fail — simply switch to manual entry to resolve this.
- During filing season, you can visit your 税務署 for in-person assistance. Staff can guide you through the process on the spot. We recommend booking ahead via **LINE** to avoid long wait times.

### Side Income Under ¥200,000

If your side income is under ¥200,000, you're exempt from filing 確定申告 for income tax purposes — but **you still need to file a separate 住民税 (resident tax) return**. Don't skip this step.
