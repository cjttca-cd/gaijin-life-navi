# Prescription Medicine Guide for Foreigners in Japan

Japan's pharmaceutical system works quite differently from many other countries. Understanding how prescriptions work can save you money — and a lot of stress when you need medication fast.

---

## 1. How the Prescription System Works

In Japan, there are two ways you can receive your medication:

- **Dispensed at an external pharmacy (院外処方)**: Your doctor gives you a 処方箋, and you take it to a 調剤薬局 (dispensing pharmacy) to fill it. This is the most common approach.
- **Dispensed in-clinic (院内処方)**: Some クリニック dispense medication directly on-site — no trip to a separate pharmacy needed. This saves you the dispensing fee and spares you the hassle of getting around when you're not feeling well.

## 2. 処方箋 Validity Period

A 処方箋 is valid for **4 days including the day it was issued** — weekends and public holidays count too.

> Example: If you get your 処方箋 on a Friday (金曜日), **Monday (月曜日) is your last day** to fill it.

After it expires, you'll need another appointment and will be charged a 再診料 (return visit fee). It's best to head to a nearby 調剤薬局 soon after your appointment — it doesn't have to be the pharmacy attached to the clinic; any pharmacy nearby will do.

---

## 3. Generic Drugs (ジェネリック医薬品) — Refusing Them May Cost You More

ジェネリック (後発医薬品) contain the same active ingredients as brand-name drugs but can cost less than half the price.

⚠️ **Important change from October 2024**: To encourage ジェネリック use, the Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare (厚生労働省) introduced an additional cost-sharing scheme (選定療養). If a generic equivalent exists and you still choose the brand-name drug, you'll need to **pay out of pocket for a quarter of the price difference**.

Because of this, **pharmacists will typically explain your ジェネリック options and suggest switching**. As long as your 処方箋 doesn't say 「変更不可」 (no substitution allowed), you can generally switch to a generic.

> To automatically get generics every time: write 「ジェネリック希望」 in your お薬手帳 — you won't need to say it again at the counter.

**Exception**: If your doctor marks 「変更不可」 on medical grounds, no extra charge applies.

## 4. Which Medications Require a Prescription in Japan?

The following cannot be bought at a ドラッグストア — you'll need a 処方箋:

| Medication | Notes |
|------------|-------|
| Antibiotics (抗菌薬) | No OTC antibiotics exist in Japan whatsoever |
| Oral contraceptives (ピル) | Requires a 産婦人科 visit for a prescription |
| Sleeping pills | Prescription required; only mild OTC sleep aids available at ドラッグストア |

## 5. ADHD Medications — Know Before You Travel

If you're currently taking ADHD medication overseas, there are some critical things to know before coming to Japan:

- **Adderall and Vyvanse are illegal in Japan** under the 覚せい剤取締法 (Stimulant Drug Control Act) — carrying or possessing them is a criminal offense, even with a valid prescription
- Legal ADHD medications in Japan include コンサータ and others, but prescribing rights are restricted — not all doctors can issue these
- **Do not bring Adderall or similar drugs into Japan** — the consequences are severe

---

## 6. Rules for Bringing Medication into Japan

When bringing medication from abroad, keep these rules in mind:

- Prescription drugs can generally be brought in for **up to 1 month's supply** (no prior declaration needed)
- Medications containing pseudoephedrine (found in some cold and allergy drugs) require a **薬監証明** if you bring more than the allowed amount
- **Best practice**: Keep medication in its original packaging and carry an English prescription or doctor's note
- If in doubt, contact your regional 厚生局 or customs (税関) in advance to confirm
