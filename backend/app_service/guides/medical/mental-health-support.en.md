# Mental Health Support Guide for Foreign Residents in Japan

Life in Japan isn't always easy — language barriers, cultural differences, loneliness, workplace stress. These are some of the most common mental health challenges foreign residents face. Japan does have a mental health support system, but it works quite differently from many other countries. Without knowing the ropes, it's easy to go in circles or pay more than you need to.

---

## 1. Two Types of Mental Health Care in Japan

When it comes to seeking mental health support in Japan, there are two main paths — and they differ significantly in cost and insurance coverage:

| Type | Japanese Name | Can Prescribe Medication | Health Insurance Covered |
|------|--------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| Psychiatrist / Psychosomatic medicine doctor | 精神科・心療内科 | ✅ | ✅ Yes |
| Counselor / Therapist | 公認心理師・臨床心理士 | ❌ | ❌ Usually out-of-pocket |

**Important note:** The "talking therapy" (counseling) that's common in many countries is generally **not covered by health insurance in Japan**. Insurance only covers appointments with a psychiatrist — typically 5–15 minutes, focused mainly on medication management. If you're primarily looking for someone to talk to, make sure to check the costs upfront.

## 2. Booking Your First Appointment: Expect a Wait

Psychiatric and psychosomatic medicine clinics almost always require appointments — **walk-ins are generally not accepted**:

- **Standard first appointment:** Typically a **2–4 week** wait
- **Clinics with foreign language services:** The wait can stretch to **1–2 months**

**Practical tips:**
- Contact multiple clinics at once and ask about waiting lists (キャンセル待ち)
- If you need immediate emotional support while waiting, these hotlines can help:

| Hotline | Number | Service |
|---------|--------|---------|
| よりそいホットライン | **0120-279-338** | 24 hours, multilingual |
| TELL Lifeline (English) | **03-5774-0992** | English mental health support |

## 3. Finding Support in Your Language

### Phone & Online Support
- **よりそいホットライン** (0120-279-338): 24-hour multilingual support line — great for immediate help during a crisis
- **TELL** (03-5774-0992): Mental health support in English, with both in-person and online counseling options

### Finding a Counselor Who Speaks Your Language
- **Urara (うらら)**: An online platform where you can search for counselors by qualification, specialty, and language
- **Online remote counseling**: Video sessions with a counselor in your home country are a practical option — no language barrier, and usually cheaper than in-person sessions in Japan

## 4. 自立支援医療: Cut Your Costs to One-Third

This is a program that **many foreign residents don't know about** — and it's well worth applying for:

- **What it does:** Reduces your out-of-pocket cost for psychiatric outpatient care from the usual **30% down to just 10%**
- **Where to apply:** The **障害福祉** (disability welfare) counter at your local city/ward office
- **Key points:**
  - The benefit takes effect **from the date you apply** (even if approval takes time, the reduced rate is backdated to your application date)
  - Many foreign residents miss out simply because they don't know it exists — doctors don't always mention it, so **ask your clinic proactively**

---

## 5. Support Available Through Your Workplace

If your struggles are related to work stress, these programs are worth knowing about:

- **産業医 consultation:** Companies with **50 or more employees** are required to have a 産業医 (occupational physician) — employees can consult them for **free** about health and workplace stress
- **傷病手当金:** Employees enrolled in company health insurance (社会保険) who take leave due to mental health issues can receive approximately **two-thirds of their salary** for up to **1 year and 6 months**
- **EAP (Employee Assistance Program):** Some companies offer free counseling through external specialists — check with your HR department to see if this applies to you
