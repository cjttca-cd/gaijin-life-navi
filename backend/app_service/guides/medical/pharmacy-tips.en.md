# Using Pharmacies in Japan: A Practical Guide for Foreigners

Japan's prescription drug system and pharmacy usage rules differ from many countries. Understanding these rules can help you save money and avoid unnecessary trouble caused by expired prescriptions or illegal medication transport.

---

## New Rules for Generic Drugs (ジェネリック)

**⚠️ October 2024 Policy Change**: 厚生労働省 has strengthened its generic drug promotion policy. If a patient wishes to use a brand-name drug (先発品), they will need to pay out-of-pocket for 1/4 of the price difference compared to the generic (the "選定療養" system). This means pharmacists are now obligated to explain the cost difference and will effectively recommend generic drugs.

- Generic drugs are typically priced more than 50% lower than brand-name drugs
- To request a generic, simply say "ジェネリックにしてください"
- Noting "ジェネリック希望" in your お薬手帳 (medication notebook) means you won't need to explain this every time
- When a prescription is marked "変更不可" or there is a medical reason, this system does not apply

## Prescription Validity Period

A 処方箋 (prescription) is valid for **4 days including the day of issue** (including holidays). For example, a prescription received on Friday expires on Monday.

If it expires, you will need to return to the clinic and pay a follow-up consultation fee. It is recommended to visit a 調剤薬局 (dispensing pharmacy) as soon as possible after receiving your prescription. Any 調剤薬局 can fill it — you are not limited to the "門前薬局" near the hospital.

## Medications That Require a Prescription

The following medications cannot be purchased directly at convenience stores or drugstores in Japan:

- Antibiotics (all types)
- Oral contraceptives (ピル) — requires a visit to 産婦人科 for a prescription
- Sleeping pills (except mild OTC products such as ドリエル)

## Special Notes on ADHD Medications

⚠️ **Adderall and Vyvanse are illegal in Japan** (violating the 覚せい剤取締法) — possession is illegal and bringing them into Japan is strictly prohibited.

Legally prescribed ADHD medications in Japan include コンサータ (registered prescriber system) and ストラテラ, among others. If needed, please consult a 精神科医.

## Rules for Bringing Medications from Abroad

- Prescription medications can generally be brought in up to **a 1-month supply** without advance declaration
- Medications containing pseudoephedrine (found in some cold medicines and allergy medications) may require a "薬監証明" if brought in larger quantities
- **Basic rule**: Keep the original packaging + carry an English prescription or medical certificate
- If in doubt, contact your regional 厚生局 or 税関 in advance
