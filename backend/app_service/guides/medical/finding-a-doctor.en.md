# How Foreign Residents Can Find the Right Doctor in Japan

Getting sick or injured while living in Japan is inevitable. But if you're not familiar with the Japanese healthcare system or unsure where to go, finding the right medical facility can feel overwhelming. This guide will walk you through your medical options in Japan and how to find doctors who offer services in foreign languages.

---

## Clinics vs. Hospitals

Japan's medical facilities fall into two main categories:

| Type | Japanese | Characteristics |
|-----|------|------|
| Clinic | クリニック / 診療所 | Smaller facilities (fewer than 20 beds), usually focused on a specific specialty |
| Hospital | 病院 | Larger facilities (20+ beds) with multiple departments |

**General rule of thumb:**
- Minor issues (cold, mild fever, skin problems, etc.) → Start with a クリニック
- Serious conditions or specialist exams → 病院
- Going directly to a large 病院 for minor symptoms may result in an extra charge (選定療養費)

## Finding Medical Facilities That Offer Foreign Language Services

### Official Resources

1. **AMDA International Medical Information Center**
   - Phone: 03-6233-9266
   - Multilingual consultation service; can refer you to medical facilities that offer foreign-language care
   - Can also answer questions about Japan's healthcare system

2. **Local Government Medical Search Websites**
   - Most local governments provide online medical facility search tools
   - Filter by language, department, and area

3. **Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare Multilingual Materials**
   - Provides multilingual reference materials you can use during medical consultations

### Certification Marks

Look for facilities with these certifications — they generally have strong experience with international patients:
- **JMIP** (外国人患者受入れ医療機関認証)
- **JIH** (Japan International Hospitals)

## Choosing the Right Department (診療科)

Clinics in Japan typically specialize in a specific 診療科. Common ones include:

| Symptom | Department | Japanese |
|-----|------|------|
| Fever, cold, stomach pain | Internal Medicine | 内科（ないか） |
| Skin issues | Dermatology | 皮膚科（ひふか） |
| Bone or joint problems | Orthopedics | 整形外科（せいけいげか） |
| Eye problems | Ophthalmology | 眼科（がんか） |
| Ear, nose, and throat | ENT | 耳鼻咽喉科（じびいんこうか） |
| Women's health, pregnancy | OB/GYN | 産婦人科（さんふじんか） |

If you're unsure which department to visit, start with 内科 (internal medicine) — it works similarly to a general practitioner.

---

## Making Appointments and the Consultation Process

- **クリニック**: Many accept walk-ins on the day, though some require appointments
- **病院**: Appointments are usually required; some larger 病院 also require a referral letter (紹介状) from a clinic

Please bring the following to your appointment:
- Health insurance card (保険証) or マイナンバーカード
- Photo ID (residence card)
- Past medical records or a list of medications you're currently taking, if applicable
