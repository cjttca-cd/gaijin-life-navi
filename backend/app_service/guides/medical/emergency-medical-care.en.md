# Emergency Medical Care Guide for Foreigners in Japan

A language barrier can become a life-threatening obstacle when a medical emergency strikes in Japan. This guide walks you through what to do in a crisis — from calling emergency services to finding a hospital, step by step.

---

## 🚨 Life-Threatening Emergency: Call 119 Immediately

If you experience any of the following, **call 119 right away** (救急車 — ambulance):

- Chest pain or difficulty breathing
- Severe bleeding or major trauma
- Loss of consciousness or seizures
- Suspected stroke (facial drooping, arm weakness, slurred speech)
- Severe allergic reaction (throat swelling, shortness of breath)
- A serious accident or rapidly worsening symptoms

📞 **After the call connects, say:**

> 「救急です。＿＿＿に来てください。」  
> Pronunciation: *Kyūkyū desu. ＿＿＿ ni kite kudasai.*  
> (Meaning: "I need an ambulance. Please come to _____ [state your address].")

**Can't speak Japanese?** Say **"English please"** — many 119 operators have interpretation support.

## 📋 Emergency Contacts at a Glance

| Situation | Number |
|-----------|--------|
| Ambulance (life-threatening) | **119** |
| Police (accident/crime) | **110** |
| Emergency consultation (unsure if you need an ambulance) | **#7119** ※ some areas only |
| Multilingual medical assistance (AMDA) | **03-6233-9266** |
| Mental health crisis support (TELL) | **03-5774-0992** |

### About #7119
**#7119** (救急安心センター) is a non-emergency consultation line — ideal when your symptoms aren't severe but you're unsure whether to call an ambulance. Note that this service isn't available in all prefectures.

## 🏥 Finding an Emergency Hospital

If you can get to a hospital on your own, these official resources can help you find facilities with foreign-language support:

- **AMDA Multilingual Medical Assistance** → [amdamedicalcenter.com](https://www.amdamedicalcenter.com/)
- **JNTO Medical Institutions Guide** (English) → [jnto.go.jp/emergency](https://www.jnto.go.jp/emergency/eng/mi_guide.html)
- **Himawari Tokyo Emergency Hospital Search** (multilingual) → [himawari.metro.tokyo.jp](https://www.himawari.metro.tokyo.jp/qq/qq13enmnlt.asp)

## 📌 What to Bring to the Hospital

### Bring these if you can

- **保険証** (health insurance card)  
  ※ After the December 2024 reform, traditional 保険証 cards are being phased out — check whether you need a マイナンバーカード or a 資格確認書 instead
- ID (passport, etc.)
- Any medications you're currently taking, or your 処方箋 (prescription)

### Health Insurance in Japan

If you're enrolled in Japanese health insurance (国民健康保険 or 社会保険), you typically pay only 30% of medical costs. Without insurance, you'll pay the full amount — and emergency care can be expensive. It's worth confirming your coverage status before you ever need it.

### Emergency vs. Regular Clinic

- **Emergency (救急外来)**: Life-threatening situations; open 24 hours
- **Clinic (クリニック)**: General illness; usually requires a 予約 (appointment) and may be closed during off-hours
