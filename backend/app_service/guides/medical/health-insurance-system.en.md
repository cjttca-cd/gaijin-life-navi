# Health Insurance System Guide for Foreign Residents in Japan

Living in Japan comes with a legal obligation to enroll in public health insurance — and doing so is the key to keeping your out-of-pocket medical costs manageable. Whether you're a full-time employee, international student, or self-employed, anyone with a valid residence status (在留資格) in Japan is required to join Japan's public health insurance system (国民皆保険).

---

## 1. Types of Health Insurance

Japan's public health insurance falls into three main categories:

| Insurance Type | Who It Covers | Premium | Out-of-Pocket |
|----------------|---------------|---------|---------------|
| **国民健康保険 (NHI)** | Self-employed, students, unemployed, etc. | Income-based | 30% |
| **社会保険 (EHI)** | Company employees | ~50% paid by employer | 30% |
| **後期高齢者医療** | Age 75 and above | Income-based | 10%–30% |

> **Which insurance you join depends on your employment status.** Company employees are typically enrolled in 社会保険 by their employer. If you're self-employed, a student, or in a transitional period after leaving a job, you'll need to enroll in 国民健康保険 yourself at your local 役所.

## 2. Enrolling in 国民健康保険

If you're a student, self-employed, or no longer covered by your company's insurance, you'll need to enroll in 国民健康保険 yourself.

- **Where to go:** Your local 役所 (city/ward office), at the health insurance counter
- **What to bring:** 在留カード (residence card), and typically your passport as well
- **When to enroll:** You can apply at the same time as your 住民登録 (resident registration). If you leave a job or lose 社会保険 coverage, head to the 役所 as soon as possible.

> ⚠️ **Medical costs during any gap in coverage must be paid in full out of pocket.** When you do enroll, you may also be required to pay back premiums for the uncovered period. Don't delay.

## 3. Your Insurance Card — The マイナ保険証

⚠️ **December 2024 Policy Change:** The traditional physical 保険証 (insurance card) has been officially discontinued. There are now two accepted methods:

- **マイナ保険証:** Using your マイナンバーカード (My Number Card) as proof of insurance at medical facilities (the standard method going forward)
- **資格確認書:** For those unable to use a マイナ保険証, you can request a 資格確認書 from your insurer as an alternative

When visiting a medical facility, bring your マイナンバーカード (or 資格確認書). If you haven't obtained your マイナンバーカード yet, it's a good idea to do so soon.

---

## 4. High-Cost Medical Expense Protection (高額療養費制度)

Japan has a system that caps how much you pay out-of-pocket in a single month — any expenses above the limit can be reimbursed.

- The monthly cap varies depending on your income level
- If you apply in advance for a **限度額適用認定証**, your payments are capped directly at the counter, so you won't need to claim a reimbursement afterward
- **Where to apply:** 国民健康保険 enrollees should go to their 役所; 社会保険 enrollees should contact their company's HR department or 健保組合 (health insurance association)

## 5. Medical Care Without Insurance

If you have no insurance coverage, you must pay **100% of your medical costs out of pocket.** The total is typically far higher than the 30% co-pay under insurance, and you won't be eligible for the 高額療養費制度 protections. Always confirm your insurance status before visiting a clinic or hospital.
