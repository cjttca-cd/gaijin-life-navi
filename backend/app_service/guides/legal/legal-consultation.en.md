# Legal Consultation Guide for Foreign Residents in Japan

Running into a legal problem in Japan can be overwhelming — language barriers, unfamiliar systems, and worries about cost make it hard to know where to start. But Japan does have legal consultation services specifically for foreign residents. The key is knowing *who* to turn to, *how* to reach them, and *how to prepare* — so you can actually protect your rights when it counts.

---

## Three Types of Legal Professionals: Know Who to Go To First

Japan has three main types of legal professionals, each with different authorities. Going to the wrong one can waste precious time and even delay your case.

- **弁護士 (licensed attorney)**: The only professional who can represent you in legal disputes. Labor conflicts, divorce litigation, criminal cases — anything involving a genuine "dispute" requires a 弁護士.
- **行政書士 (administrative scrivener)**: Handles administrative procedures like visa applications, but **cannot represent you in disputes or give legal advice**. Some foreign residents have mistakenly brought cases that needed a 弁護士 to a 行政書士, causing costly delays.
- **司法書士 (judicial scrivener)**: Handles specific procedures like real estate registration, but has more limited authority than a 弁護士.

**The core rule: if there's a "dispute," go to a 弁護士. For paperwork and procedures only, a 行政書士 or 司法書士 may be enough.**

Not sure which one applies to your situation? Start with a free legal consultation at your local municipal office and simply ask — figuring out who you need to talk to is exactly what free consultations are for.

## Free and Low-Cost Legal Consultation Options

### National Legal Support Organization

Japan has a national legal support organization that offers multilingual services for foreigners. Before you go, here are a few things to know so you're not caught off guard:

- **Multilingual information ≠ legal advice**: They mainly explain systems and rights through interpretation. Actual legal advice still requires a separate consultation with a 弁護士.
- **Fee advance ≠ free**: Legal fees can be covered upfront by the organization, but you'll need to repay them in monthly installments afterward. Recipients of public assistance (生活保護) may qualify for a waiver.
- **Some visa statuses are not eligible**: People who are overstaying their visa generally cannot access civil legal aid.
- **You can't choose your attorney**: Attorneys are assigned by the organization, so the match may not be a specialist in your type of case.

### Free Consultations at Local Municipal Offices

Many municipalities hold periodic free legal consultations. You can often book an interpreter at the same time, at no cost. Great for getting your bearings and figuring out your next step.

### Your Country's Embassy or Consulate

Your home country's embassy or consulate may keep a list of attorneys who speak your language. Don't hesitate to reach out and ask.

### Attorney Search Services and Word-of-Mouth

Attorney search services let you filter by language and specialty, making it easier to find a 弁護士 with multilingual capability. That said, personal referrals through community networks — Chinese or Korean associations, social media, churches, mosques — are often the most reliable way to find a trustworthy attorney. **Word-of-mouth recommendations tend to produce the best results.** Try consulting 2–3 attorneys before making a decision.

## Preparing for Your Consultation: Make the Most of 30 Minutes

Free legal consultations are typically around 30 minutes. A little preparation goes a long way:

1. **Write a one-page summary of what happened**: Timeline, people involved, key events
2. **Bring all relevant documents**: Contracts, pay stubs, email screenshots, chat logs — the more complete, the better
3. **Clarify two things in advance**: ① What is the core issue, and ② What outcome are you hoping for
4. **Take notes during the consultation**: Use your phone or paper, and **make sure to ask "what should I do next?"**

**Important for foreign residents**: Always bring your **在留カード (Residence Card)**. Your visa status directly affects how your case should be handled — it's essential information for the 弁護士 to advise you properly.
